Topological Signal Detection: Research Documentation

1. Motivation

Classical signal detection theories assume signal stationarity over the observation window. Standard matched filters and Fast Fourier Transforms (FFT) lose significant efficacy (SNR collapse) when coherent signals undergo arbitrary frequency drift, modulation, or finite-coherence damping. We explore Nonlinear Topological Data Analysis (TDA)—specifically Takens delay embedding coupled with Vietoris-Rips persistent homology—as a robust alternative for detecting highly noisy, non-stationary periodic signals.

2. Why TDA?

A periodic signal, regardless of its instantaneous frequency drift ($f(t) = f_0 + \dot{f}t$) or phase noise, natively traces out a closed 1-dimensional cycle (a "circle") in its phase space. Additive noise simply thickens this circular manifold, while drift non-uniformly parameterizes it. Persistent homology is mathematically invariant to continuous deformation, making it structurally immune to the energy-smearing that plagues classical Fourier estimators under drift scenarios.

3. Signal Model

The generalized discrete time-series observable $x[n]$ is given by:

$x[n] = A[n] \sin\left(2\pi \int (f_0 + \dot{f}t)dt\right) + w[n]$

where $A[n]$ simulates coherence degradation, $\dot{f}$ represents the linear frequency drift rate, and $w[n]$ corresponds to additive noise (Gaussian, Impulsive, or Pink).

4. Delay Embedding

To reconstruct the underlying dynamical attractor from a scalar time-series, we utilize Takens' Delay Embedding Theorem.

Implementation note: For computational efficiency in this web-based interactive demonstrator, we implement a 2D embedding space $X_n = [x(t), x(t+\tau)]$. Real-world drifting, non-linear, or multimodal systems generally require a 3D (or higher) embedding dimension ($D \ge 3$) according to Whitney's Embedding Theorem ($D = 2M+1$ where $M$ is the manifold dimension).

5. Vietoris-Rips Persistence

We construct a Vietoris-Rips simplicial complex over points sampled from the phase space embedding. Because standard VR computation scales exponentially with the number of points, we adopt a greedy Min-Max Landmark sub-sampling (K=40) before constructing the persistent complex, evaluating birth/death intervals of 1-dimensional homology groups ($H_1$).

6. Detection Metrics

The Naive DFT Score calculates the signal-to-noise ratio within a target discrete frequency bin over $O(N^2)$ algorithmic complexity (true FFT implementations execute in $O(N \log N)$ but require robust window padding).
The STDFT Score evaluates windowed frequency bands representing Short-Time Discrete Fourier Transform mitigations.
The TDA Score returns the summation or maximal interval of the $H_1$ persistence lifetimes ($Death_i - Birth_i$).

7. Dark Matter Axion Proxy (Study 3 Disclaimer)
